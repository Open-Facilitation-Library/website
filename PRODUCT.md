# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three audiences arrive at openfac.org:

1. **Facilitators and deliberation researchers.** Practitioners and academics working on group sensemaking, deliberative democracy, dispute resolution, research interviews. They want to know what OFL standardizes and whether the methods are rigorous and usable.
2. **Civic-tech and AI builders.** Engineers building AI facilitation into platforms. They want portable specs, skills, and evals they can implement, and proof it is vendor-neutral (not locked to one product).
3. **Funders and contributors.** People deciding whether to support or join (via Giveth, GitHub, Substack). They need to grasp the mission and the momentum quickly.

Context: arriving from a paper citation, a Substack post, a conference mention, or a Harmonica reference. Reading on a laptop, daytime, judging credibility and relevance in under a minute.

## Product Purpose

openfac.org is the front door to the Open Facilitation Library: an open, vendor-neutral commons of standards, skills, evals, and research for AI-assisted facilitation and deliberative democracy. The depth already lives at wiki.openfac.org and across the GitHub org. This landing page explains what OFL is in ten seconds, frames its relationship to Harmonica (reference implementation and founding contributor, not owner), and routes people onward. Success is a newcomer who understands OFL and takes one next step: read the wiki, browse a repo, contribute, or fund.

## Brand Personality

Open, rigorous, civic. The voice of a research commons, not a product pitch: confident about the work, generous with it, plain-spoken. It treats the reader as a peer (a facilitator, researcher, or builder), explains without dumbing down, and never oversells. Calm and credible over hype.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing.** No hero-metric block, no pricing tiers, no "trusted by" logo wall, no grid of identical icon-heading-blurb feature cards.
- **Harmonica clone.** OFL is vendor-neutral; the page must not read as a Harmonica marketing surface or borrow Harmonica's locked Stone-only product design language.
- **Web3 / crypto aesthetic.** Despite Giveth funding, no neon-on-black, no token energy.
- **Over-animated startup splash.** Motion is restrained and purposeful.

## Design Principles

1. **Vendor-neutral by construction.** Harmonica appears as one of many (one of 14 documented platforms) and as the reference implementation, never as the owner. The structure itself signals independence.
2. **Route, do not duplicate.** The wiki and repos hold the depth; the landing's job is clarity and a confident hand-off, not re-explaining everything.
3. **Show the layers.** OFL is a real structure (Skills, Workflows, Synthesis, plus Evals and Cross-Pollination). Make the architecture legible, not a vague mission statement.
4. **Commons, not company.** Language and visuals say "shared resource you can use and shape," not "product you buy."
5. **Coherent with the family.** Type, palette, and tone echo wiki.openfac.org so the whole OFL surface reads as one project.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Maintain sufficient contrast on the warm off-white background (sage green is a fill/mark color, not body text; use a darkened sage for any text use). Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Semantic landmarks, full keyboard navigation, descriptive link text (the page is mostly outbound links, so link text must carry meaning on its own). Body type at 16px or larger with a 65 to 75 character measure.
