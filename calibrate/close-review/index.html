OFL — Facilitation close review (demo)

DEMO — this is a live preview of the Open Facilitation Library close-review labeling surface. The turns below are invented for the demo, not real sessions. To label a real calibration pack, message your OFL coordinator.

For each closing moment below, is this a good close? Mark Pass or Fail and give one line on why, in your own words. Trust your practice. There are no trick questions, and disagreement with the stated criterion is welcome (there is a box for that at the bottom).

How this becomes calibration: each labeled turn becomes a few-shot example in an LLM judge's prompt. A judge with zero labels tends to over-approve fluent-but-generic facilitation, because a general-purpose model's default taste rewards fluency. A judge trained on labels from practicing facilitators scores like the labelers do. That is what the Open Facilitation Library is calibrating: the judgment behind the number, not just the number.

The judge is advisory only. It flags for review; it never certifies or blocks anything. Your name is not attached to your labels unless you want credit.

This demo takes about 5 to 7 minutes. Your progress saves in the browser automatically, so you can close and come back. When done, press "Export labels.json" to see the file that would come back on a real pack.
